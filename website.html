About the call center data

All 311 Service Requests from 2010 to present. This information is automatically updated daily. 311’s mission is to provide the public with quick, easy access to all New York City government services and information while maintaining the highest possible level of customer service. 311 allows other government bodies to focus on their core missions and manage their workload efficiently. 311 also helps City agencies improve their service delivery by providing accurate and consistent data tracking and analysis of all service requests. 311 provides access to New York City government services through eight platforms: Call Center, Social Media, Mobile App, Text, Video Relay Service and TTY/text telephone.

(Source: City of New York - NYC OpenData)

This beneficial ozone has been partially destroyed by man-made chemicals. “Bad” ozone is found at ground level and is a harmful air pollutant, because of its health effects on people and the environment, and it is the main ingredient in smog.

Different State, Different Rates

The natural concentration of ozone is about 0.01 parts per million (or 10 parts per billion). According to the Environmental Protection Agency, exposure to ozone levels greater than 0.07 parts per million (or 70 parts per billion) for 8 hours is unhealthy. The highest levels of ozone from years 2013-2018 were found mainly in the southwest region of the US, with peak ozone levels measured at 0.062 parts per million (or 62 parts per billion) in Clear Creek County, Colorado. The lowest levels of ozone were found in Juncos, Puerto Rico at 0.006 parts per million (or 6 parts per billion).
